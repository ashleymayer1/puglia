Where We'd Stay

One base for all 7 nights keeps everything within a 20-40 minute drive: the trulli towns, the Adriatic beaches, and day trips to Lecce or Matera. One hotel, no repacking.

5-Star Resort Hotel

Best

A polished, full-service hotel with a spa, pool(s), multiple dining/bar options, and on-site boat excursions. A destination in itself on the chill days. Cala Ponte (below) is the priced example for this tier.

$8,000 total for 3 rooms, 7 nights (real Cala Ponte B&B member rate) → $1,333pp.

Restored Masseria w/ Pool

Better

A converted farmhouse estate with a real pool, breakfast included, comfortable beds and AC, and a lounge or terrace built for evening cocktails and a deck of cards. This is the format most local guides recommend for groups.

Roughly $6,200–7,000 total for 7 nights, split 6 ways.

Boutique Hotel / B&B

Good

A well-rated small hotel or B&B in or near Ostuni/the Valle d'Itria, breakfast included, simple and comfortable. Less lounging-at-the-property time, but still fully taken care of — no cooking, no driving.

Roughly $4,000–4,600 total for 7 nights, split 6 ways.

Example Properties We're Considering

Real, named examples pulled from current research — not all are bookings yet. Helps make the tiers concrete.

Cala Ponte, a Tribute Portfolio Hotel

Best — Priced

5-star Marriott property right in Polignano a Mare — pool, spa, Hedoné restaurant, Otium lounge bar (cocktails + that evening-hangout vibe), and they run their own boat excursions from the same stretch of coast as our boat day. $373/night/room, garden-view king, B&B member rate for 7+ nights → $7,833 for 3 rooms, 7 nights (used as $8,000 in the Best-tier total). One caveat worth knowing: a recent TripAdvisor review flagged the hotel's location next to a busy highway, with noise audible from some rooms/balconies — worth asking about room placement when booking.

Masseria Il Frantoio

Better — Masseria

Historic 17th-century working masseria near Ostuni, rooms from around $200/night, citrus and olive groves, a well-regarded set-menu dinner with an estate tour included. Vs. Cala Ponte: smaller and more intimate (a handful of rooms vs. a full resort), one dining venue instead of several, no spa or lounge bar — but roughly half the nightly cost and a more authentic, working-farm character. Cala Ponte is the polished resort experience; Il Frantoio is the storied-property experience.

Masseria Cervarolo / Masseria La Carrube

Better — Masseria

Both in the $200/night range per local guides, countryside settings in the Valle d'Itria, pools, breakfast included. Solid Better-tier candidates worth getting direct quotes from.

Borgo Canonica

Good — Boutique

Trulli-style boutique stay in the Valle d'Itria countryside, around $200–250/night, well-reviewed and charming without resort pricing.

B&B Murex (Bari) or similar in Ostuni

Good — B&B

Smaller, well-rated B&Bs in Bari or central Ostuni run roughly $100-150/night — useful as a Good-tier benchmark, especially if the group prefers being in-town over countryside.

✨
Nicer than the Tribute? Yes — beyond Marriott, Puglia's actual top tier Cala Ponte is a genuinely good 5-star, but it's mid-pack among Puglia's named luxury properties, not the ceiling. If "nicest possible" matters more than Marriott status, these consistently top the rankings: Borgo Egnazia (Savelletri) — the big one: Michelin-starred Due Camini, four pools, two beach clubs, a golf course; rates start around $317/night for an entry room but climb well beyond that for nicer categories in May. Masseria San Domenico (Savelletri) — the most "rooted," historic masseria of the group, golf course, Aveda spa in natural caves, large saltwater pool. All four would likely price above Cala Ponte's $373/night rate in May — probably $450-700+/night/room depending on category.

Where We'd Eat

For the meals not at the property — a few well-reviewed, real options by town and time of day. Reservations recommended in May, especially for dinner.

☕ Breakfast / Coffee

  • Burro Café (Ostuni) — coffee, Viennese pastries, a relaxed start before a town day
  • La Gilda at Taverna Gelosia (Ostuni) — a more substantial breakfast beyond the usual cornetto
  • Most masserie/hotels include breakfast on-site — easiest default on chill days

🍽️ Lunch

  • Pescaria (Polignano a Mare) — Italy's first "fish fast food," casual seafood sandwiches, great for a boat-day-adjacent lunch
  • Komera (Ostuni) — cute, breezy, good for a casual midday stop in town
  • Sapere & Sapori (Ostuni area) — orecchiette and local greens, simple and excellent

🍷 Dinner

  • Grotta Palazzese (Polignano a Mare) — the iconic cave restaurant over the sea; a true splurge, book well ahead, ideal for the celebratory night
  • Osteria del Tempo Perso (Ostuni) — dinner in a centuries-old grotto in the historic center, a must-do per nearly every local guide
  • Il Posto Affianco (Ostuni) — modern Apulian plates plus a Neapolitan pizza menu, intimate and a little dressier
  • Trattoria Le Zie (Lecce) — traditional, generous, beloved by locals — good fit for the Lecce day trip

🍸 Aperitivo / Evening

  • Borgo Antico Bistrot (Ostuni) — sweeping sunset views over the whitewashed rooftops, spritzes and shared boards
  • Otium Lounge Bar (Cala Ponte, Polignano) — if staying there, the on-site lounge is built for exactly the cocktails-and-cards evening vibe
  • Otherwise, the property's own lounge/terrace is the default most nights, per the Costa Rica-style pacing

Suggested 7-Night Itinerary

One hotel, 7 nights, used as a true home base — mixing excursion days with true chill days, same rhythm that worked so well on the Costa Rica trip. Private drivers handle every transfer and excursion; nobody's on the hook to drive. Evenings default to a quiet lounge, cocktails, and cards back at the property.

Day 1 — Wed, May 12
✈️ Fly RDU → Bari, Check In
Depart Tue evening May 11 from RDU, connect through Frankfurt (or another hub) for the overnight transatlantic leg in premium economy. Private driver meets the group at Bari airport for the ~45-60 min transfer straight to the hotel — check-in begins night 1 of 7.
Day 2 — Thu, May 13 · Chill
🏊 Recovery + Pool Day
Recovery day after the flight. Pool, sun, no agenda. Walk into the nearest village in the evening for dinner, or stay in if the property has a good restaurant.
Day 3 — Fri, May 14 · Excursion
🏘️ Alberobello & Locorotondo
Private driver for the day. Morning in the trulli of Alberobello (go early, before tour buses), afternoon in Locorotondo for rooftop views and a slow lunch. Driver waits or returns on call.
Day 4 — Sat, May 15 · Excursion
⛵ Private Boat Day — Polignano / Monopoli Coast
Driver transfer to the marina, then a full-day private charter exploring the sea caves, Lama Monachile, and Cala Corvino, with swim stops and an onboard aperitivo. Departs from Cala Ponte Marina (Polignano) or Margherita Pier (Monopoli).
Day 5 — Sun, May 16 · Chill
💆 Pool & Spa Day at the Property
No driver, no itinerary. Pool, optional spa treatments (Best tier), a long lazy lunch on-site. This is the "read by the pool" day.
Day 6 — Mon, May 17 · Excursion
🤍 Ostuni + Lecce
Private driver for a full day: Ostuni's whitewashed lanes in the morning, then on to Lecce for the baroque cathedral, Santa Croce, and pasticciotto pastries. Dinner out before the drive back. (Matera is a solid swap if the group prefers cave-dwellings over baroque churches.)
Day 7 — Tue, May 18 · Chill
🛋️ Last Full Day — Pool, Maybe a Market, Final Night
A last easy day at the property — pool, optional short stroll into a nearby village or olive oil tasting if anyone's restless. Lounge, cocktails, and cards for the final evening, last night at the hotel.
Day 8 — Wed, May 19
🛫 Check Out + Fly Home
Checkout after breakfast, private driver to Bari airport for the morning departure. Short hop to the hub, then premium economy transatlantic back to RDU — home by evening.

What You're Getting

⛵ The Boat Day

  • Private charter (not shared) — sailboat, motor yacht, or small catamaran depending on group preference
  • Skipper + hostess, aperitivo, snorkel gear and SUP typically included
  • Coastline from Polignano a Mare to Monopoli: sea caves, cliffs, swim stops in hidden coves
  • Full day (~7hrs) recommended for a group celebration, with lunch aboard on Better/Best

🚐 Getting Around — Fully Driven

  • No rental car, no designated driver — a private driver handles every airport transfer and excursion day
  • Comfortable minivan or sedan depending on tier, booked per excursion rather than for the whole week
  • Chill days at the property need no transport at all
  • Better/Best tiers include evening car service for dinners out, so wine with dinner is never a problem

🍝 The Food — All Out, No Cooking

  • Every meal is at a restaurant, trattoria, or catered at the property — orecchiette, burrata from Andria, bombette in Cisternino, fresh Adriatic seafood
  • Breakfast included at the property on all three tiers
  • Reservations recommended in high season for sought-after restaurants

🛋️ The Evenings

  • Quiet lounge or terrace at the property most nights — cocktails, cards, low-key hang time, same vibe as Costa Rica
  • Masseria and resort properties (Better/Best) are built for exactly this — shaded courtyards, lounge furniture, a bar
  • Dinner out on excursion days, in or at the property on chill days

Don't Forget These

🚐 Book Drivers Early Private driver demand peaks in May — worth locking in the boat-day transfer and the two excursion days as soon as we have firm dates.
💳 Cash & Currency Italy uses euros. Cards are widely accepted, but small trattorias and markets often prefer cash — handy for driver tips too.
😴 Book Seats Together Premium economy cabins are small on widebody aircraft — book seats as a group immediately after purchasing to make sure we're together for the long crossing.
🌊 Boat Day Weather Flexibility Sea conditions can shift the boat day — most operators offer free rescheduling, worth confirming when booking.
🛂 Passports Valid 6+ months past return date (past Nov 19, 2027). Renew now if needed — processing backlogs have been long.

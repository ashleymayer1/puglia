The Five Properties

All five have pools, restaurants, and bars. What varies is location (rural countryside vs. coastal/walkable), scale, and how the chill days feel.

🫒
A word on Puglia geography Masserias — the traditional fortified farmhouses that define this region — are always in the countryside. They're never walkable to a town or restaurant; that's not a flaw, it's the format. You're in the olive groves with nowhere to be, and a private driver handles every evening dinner run (already in the costs). The one exception in this list: La Sommità sits inside Ostuni's pedestrian historic center — out the front door and you're in the lanes.
Best

Masseria Torre Coccaro

Best · Savelletri di Fasano · Leading Hotels of the World · ~$517/room/night

A 16th-century fortified farmhouse turned full-service luxury resort — the option that resolves the tradeoffs in the other tiers. Aveda spa set in natural caves (Turkish bath, sauna, indoor pool, treatments). Two pools. Private beach club via free shuttle May through September. Free shuttle to Savelletri, a small fishing village with excellent seafood restaurants — so evenings out don't require advance planning. Restaurant Egnathia draws from the masseria's own organic farm. 44 rooms and suites, many with private gardens or plunge pools. Not in a town, but not isolated either.

🏊Pools: Large outdoor pool with sun loungers + heated indoor spa pool; poolside bar
🌊Beach: Free shuttle to Coccaro Beach Club (private sandy beach, restaurant, loungers) — 2.5 miles; May–Sept
🚐Town access: Free shuttle to Savelletri village (seafood dinners, casual atmosphere); 15 min to Fasano town
💆Spa: Aveda spa in actual caves — Turkish bath, sauna, indoor pool, yoga, pilates, massages

Where We'd Eat

Breakfasts at the property every morning. Everything else is out — a mix of dinners at the hotel on chill days and restaurants in town on excursion days, with one proper celebratory dinner. A few real, well-reviewed picks by category.

☕ Coffee & Morning

  • Breakfast included at the property every morning — no decisions required before coffee
  • Burro Café (Ostuni) — excuse to wander if you're staying near town; Viennese pastries and proper espresso
  • Caffè Garibaldi (Ostuni piazza) — local favorite on the main square; cornetto and cappuccino, people-watching included

🍽️ Lunch

  • Pescaria (Polignano a Mare) — Italy's first "fish fast food," casual seafood sandwiches and plates, perfect for a boat-day lunch in town
  • Sapere & Sapori (near Ostuni) — orecchiette with local greens, beautifully simple and good
  • Lunch aboard the boat — included in the full-day charter; the best lunch of the trip
  • Slow lunch in Locorotondo or Lecce on excursion days — let the driver suggest a local spot

🍷 Dinner

  • Grotta Palazzese (Polignano) — the cave restaurant over the sea; under new direction since 2025 (chef Martino Ruggieri, two Michelin stars). Book months ahead. (~€130–200/pp — budgeted separately; see notes)
  • Osteria del Tempo Perso (Ostuni) — dinner inside a medieval cave-grotto in the historic center; almost universally recommended by local guides
  • Osteria Ricanatti (Ostuni) — the locals' pick; handmade pasta, unpretentious, great value
  • Trattoria Le Zie (Lecce) — traditional, generous, beloved; the natural dinner after the Lecce excursion day

🍸 Aperitivo & Evening

  • Borgo Antico Bistrot (Ostuni) — sweeping sunset views over the whitewashed rooftops; spritzes and shared boards
  • Otium Lounge Bar at Cala Ponte — on-site bar built for long evenings; the cocktails-and-cards spot if staying there
  • The property's own terrace or courtyard covers most evenings — every property on this list has outdoor lounge space and a bar

Suggested 7-Night Itinerary

One hotel, 7 nights, true home base. Three excursion days, three chill days, one boat day — the same alternating rhythm as Costa Rica. Private drivers handle every transfer. Evenings default to the property lounge, cocktails, and cards.

Tue, May 11 — evening departure from RDU
✈️ Fly RDU → Frankfurt
Depart RDU in the afternoon or evening. Overnight transatlantic leg to Frankfurt, then a short connection into Bari — arrive midday May 12. Private driver meets the group at BRI arrivals for the 45-60 min transfer straight to the hotel.
Day 1 — Wed, May 12 · Arrive
🛬 Check In — Recovery Afternoon
Arrive midday. Check in, find the pool, do nothing in particular. Light dinner at or near the property — easy first night after travel.
Day 2 — Thu, May 13 · Chill
🏊 Full Recovery — Pool Day
No agenda. Pool, sun, whatever everyone needs after the overnight flight. Dinner out nearby or at the property.
Day 3 — Fri, May 14 · Excursion
🏘️ Alberobello & Locorotondo
Private driver for the day. Morning in the trulli of Alberobello (UNESCO-listed; go early before tour buses). Afternoon in Locorotondo for rooftop views and a slow lunch. Back to the property mid-afternoon; driver waits or returns on call.
Day 4 — Sat, May 15 · Excursion
⛵ Private Boat Day — Sea Caves of Polignano
The centerpiece day. Private driver to the marina; full-day private charter on the Adriatic — sea caves, Lama Monachile, hidden coves between Polignano and Monopoli, swimming stops, aperitivo and lunch aboard. The taste of what the Greece catamaran trip would have been.
Day 5 — Sun, May 16 · Chill
💆 Pool & Spa Day
No driver, no plan. Pool, optional spa treatments, long lunch at the property. The "read a book, do nothing" day.
Day 6 — Mon, May 17 · Excursion
🤍 Ostuni + Lecce
Private driver for a full day. Morning in Ostuni's whitewashed lanes. Afternoon in Lecce — baroque Santa Croce basilica, the cathedral, pasticciotto pastries. Dinner out in Lecce before the drive back. (Matera is a solid swap if cave-dwellings are preferred over baroque churches.)
Day 7 — Tue, May 18 · Chill
🛋️ Last Full Day
Pool, optional olive oil tasting or market browse, no commitments. Final evening — the celebratory dinner if it hasn't happened yet (Grotta Palazzese, or Osteria del Tempo Perso). Cocktails and cards after.
Day 8 — Wed, May 19 · Fly Home
🛫 Check Out + BRI → Home
Checkout after breakfast. Private driver to Bari airport. Short hop to Munich, long transatlantic leg to Newark, connect home to RDU — back by evening.

✈️ Book Flights Soon May 2027 schedules open around June–July 2026 — that window is now. Book everyone on the same flights first; anyone upgrading cabin does so on their own ticket. Watch for United's EWR–BRI re-announcement in fall 2026.

💳 Cash & Euros Cards are widely accepted, but small trattorias, markets, and driver tips often prefer cash. Bring some. ATMs are in every town center.

🌊 Boat Day Flexibility Sea conditions occasionally shift timing or route. Most operators offer free rescheduling — confirm when booking. Sea caves are best in the morning before afternoon chop.
